1. Introduction

This project delivers an end-to-end business-analytics solution on top of the Amazon Sale Report dataset — a real transactional dump from Amazon India covering April–June 2022. The work spans four layers:

  1. Automated data wrangling — programmatic cleaning of 128k+ noisy rows.
  2. Statistical exploration — descriptive stats, distributional analysis, correlation matrices, and three formal hypothesis tests.
  3. Interactive dashboard — a multi-page Streamlit application with sidebar filters, dynamic charts, geographic visualisation, and KPI cards.
  4. Predictive & generative bonus features — a Holt-Winters 30-day revenue forecast and a Claude-powered AI Insights page.

The business question the dashboard is built to answer is:

Which products, regions, and channels are driving Amazon India revenue — and where is the business losing money to cancellations?

Every cleaning decision is justified below. The full code is in notebooks/01_data_cleaning.ipynb.

6. Statistical Analysis

Three formal tests were run at α = 0.05.

Test Question Statistic p-value Conclusion
Chi-square independence Is cancellation independent of category? χ² = 61.51 (df = 8) 2.35 × 10⁻¹⁰ Reject H₀ — cancellation depends on category
One-way ANOVA Do mean order amounts differ across categories? F = 10,873.3 < 1 × 10⁻¹⁰ Reject H₀ — category strongly affects amount
Mann-Whitney U Do B2B and B2C amount distributions differ? U = 60.66 M 8 × 10⁻⁶ Reject H₀ — B2B median (₹665) > B2C median (₹612)

The Pearson and Spearman correlation matrices agree on direction; both indicate weak linear relationships between amount, quantity, and the cancellation flag (|r| < 0.25), which is what we would expect given the varied SKU mix.

  • Sales Analytics — granularity-selectable time-series, DoW × Month heatmap, correlation heatmap, stacked category × fulfilment bar, scatter, top-20 orders table, cancellation rate table.
  • Geographic — India bubble map, top states bar, top cities bar, state-level KPI table.

  • AI Insights — deterministic templated bullets + optional Claude- generated narrative based on the user's filters.

9. ML Forecasting Results

Three models were compared on a 30-day hold-out (last 30 of 91 days):

Model RMSE MAE MAPE
Naive 7-day MA 111,844 91,393 13.6 %
Linear Regression (time features) 375,720 362,992 48.3 %
Holt-Winters (additive, period=7) 104,787 89,525 12.4 %

Holt-Winters wins. It was refit on the full series and projects 30 days forward; results are written to data/forecast.csv for the dashboard.

The Augmented Dickey-Fuller test on the daily series returned p < 0.001, indicating stationarity — Holt-Winters's additive structure is appropriate.

10. AI Insights Integration

The AI Insights page combines two layers:

  1. Templated insights — pure-Python rules in src/llm_insights.py. They run with zero external dependencies and always render.
  2. LLM narrative — when ANTHROPIC_API_KEY is present in .streamlit/secrets.toml, the page sends the aggregated KPIs (no raw rows, no PII) to Claude with a system prompt that constrains the model to a 3-paragraph executive summary covering trends, risks, and recommendations.

The graceful-fallback design means the dashboard remains fully usable without an API key — a deployment requirement on Streamlit Cloud free tier.

11. Key Findings & Business Recommendations

  1. Cancellation rate of 14.2 % is materially above a healthy 5–10 % band and varies significantly by category (χ² test, p < 10⁻⁹). Recommend a focused review of the highest-cancellation lines.
  2. Revenue is in a -10.7 % MoM decline in the latest full month. The 30-day Holt-Winters forecast projects continued softness; demand- stimulation actions (promotion targeting, top-state inventory) should be considered.
  3. Set, Kurta, and Western Dress dominate revenue, with Set in the lead. ANOVA confirms category is a strong driver of basket size (F ≈ 10,800). Concentration is a risk — a single-category dip would hit topline hard.
  4. Maharashtra, Karnataka, and Tamil Nadu account for the largest revenue slices. Targeted marketing and logistics should be optimised for this trio.
  5. B2B orders have a 9 % higher median amount than B2C (Mann-Whitney U, p < 10⁻⁵). A small but real lever — worth a dedicated B2B onboarding page.
  6. Outliers are real, not errors — 1.3 % of amounts and 3.0 % of quantities flagged as outliers turn out to be high-value bulk orders. The dashboard offers an opt-in filter rather than removing them.
  7. Sunday is the strongest revenue weekday, Thursday the weakest. Worth A/B testing weekday-targeted promotion windows.

12. Limitations

  • No cost-of-goods column → cannot compute true profit; the dashboard reports Revenue and substitutes related KPIs (Cancellation %, AOV).
  • No customer_id → classic RFM segmentation is impossible. Order- level segmentation is used instead.
  • 91 days of data is a short window for time-series forecasting; the forecast is directional, not commit-grade.
  • No exogenous variables (price, marketing spend, holidays). Real production forecasting would incorporate these.
